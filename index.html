Stellar Multiplicity

More than 20 per cent of all stars exist in a multiple system, with the fraction increasing with mass to around 50 per cent for main sequence solar-type stars. Multiplicity studies have advanced significantly since the early years of binary star research. Starting from identifying single systems, and crude descriptions of small samples, we have moved to detailed studies of multiplicity statistics and its dependency on mass, metallicity and its environment.

Multiplicity of cool stars has several implications on both stellar and planetary evolution. Some of these implications are:

  • - Formation of binaries and the subsequent planet formation
  • - Dynamical effects of multiple systems and the orientation of the planetary orbits
  • - Interactions in close binary systems
  • - Detached systems and precise direct measurements of stellar properties
